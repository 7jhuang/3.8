Dehydrated vs Dry Skin (And how to treat dehydrated skin)

Skinclusive | 23/09/2021

Many people get the term "dehydrated skin" confused with dry skin. Dehydrated skin is not a skin type like oily or dry skin. It is actually a skin concern which can affect any skin type. Today we will teach you the difference between dehydrated and dry skin.

So what's the difference?

Dry skin refers to the lack of moisture in our skin in order to hold all of the hydration in. As you may know, our skin naturally produces sebum that sits on the top layer of our skin to make sure the water in our skin doesn't dry out. When we have dry skin, our skin isn't doing a proper job of producing that sebum. For dehydrated skin, it refers to not having enough of that water in our skin. We need a good balance of sebum (in this case, moisture) and water (in this case, hydration).

How do you know you have dehydrated skin? And how can you treat it?

Unfortunately, it can be quite complicated and differs for people. But for some, their skin can be producing as much oils as ever and yet still feel very flat and dull. For dry skin types, some might experience their usually dry skin with flaking, but even with moisturiser on, it's still not plump due to lack of hydration. So let's take care of this problem by taking advantage of our night routine where we can try different products and not have to worry about sunscreen or looking crazy to others.

A good idea is to try a heavier moisturiser at night. This ensures that we are tackling the dry skin problem by ensuring our skin is retaining the moisture required. We recommend the SkinFix Barrier + Lipid-Peptide Cream. Lipids ensure our skin barrier is protected. But as we age, we lose more of those lipids and our skin barrier is put at risk. This cream will help to restore those lipids and as a bonus, works well during the daytime for dry skin types!

And lastly, we recommend using a serum to help restore the hydration that dehydrated skin needs. Hyaluronic acid is a great ingredient for hydration because it pulls in water from the air into your skin. However, if you live in a very dry climate, this may not be an ideal ingredient for you as it will pull the water from out of your skin instead!
